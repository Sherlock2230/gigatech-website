# Giga Tech — Project Roadmap

Renewable energy and engineering company | Chinhoyi & Harare, Zimbabwe
Website: [gigatechzw.com](https://gigatechzw.com)

Giga Tech grows in deliberate phases. Each phase funds and builds the technical
credibility for the next, moving from field installation work toward
engineering-led energy intelligence and industrial automation. The
mechatronics engineering foundation of the founders is the differentiator
threaded through every phase.

## Phase 1 — Solar & Electrical Infrastructure *(Status: Active)*

The revenue engine. Residential and commercial solar installation plus
electrical services, delivered to certifiable standard.

**Core offerings**
- Residential solar systems (design, supply, install, maintain)
- Commercial solar systems (10kVA–60kVA tiers)
- Electrical services and fault-finding

**Planned expansion — General Electrical Installations** *(deferred, not yet on website)*
Modelled on the installation tier of established local firms (e.g. Power
Giants Pvt Ltd), structured by customer type:
- **Domestic** — new house wiring, rewiring, DB board upgrades, COC-ready installations, prepaid meter wiring
- **Commercial** — shop/office fit-outs, three-phase installations, lighting design
- **Industrial & Agricultural** — motor control panels (star-delta, motor control), borehole & irrigation pump installations, machine wiring

Strategic note: wiring is a low-margin, crowded trade. Its value here is as a
**customer acquisition channel** — installation clients become solar clients
— and as the natural showcase for mechatronics skill (control panels, motor
circuits) that bridges into Phase 3. Only services we can deliver and certify
go on the website.

**Supporting initiatives under exploration**
- Retail shop selling solar equipment and accessories
- Tender participation (enabled by company registration)

**Exit criteria for Phase 1 maturity**
- Steady pipeline of installations across at least two customer segments
- Photo/project gallery deep enough to win work on evidence, not promises
- Repeatable EPC process documented end to end

## Phase 2 — Energy Intelligence & Smart Metering *(Status: Planned)*

Layering data and monitoring on top of the installed base from Phase 1.

- Remote monitoring of installed solar systems (performance, faults, yield)
- Smart metering solutions for households and commercial clients
- Energy audits and consumption analytics as a paid service
- Dashboards / reporting for commercial clients

**Why this phase works:** every Phase 1 installation becomes a candidate for a
monitoring upsell, and the data infrastructure built here is the same stack
industrial automation runs on.

**Entry criteria:** stable Phase 1 revenue; installed base large enough that
monitoring is a real product, not a demo.

---

## Phase 3 — Industrial Automation, SCADA & IoT *(Status: Long-term)*

The destination: engineering-led automation for industrial, mining, and
agricultural clients.

- Industrial automation and process control
- SCADA system design and integration
- IoT sensor networks for agriculture and industry
- Control panel design and MCC rooms at industrial scale

**Strategic context being tracked**
- Zimbabwe–China industrial collaboration opportunities
- AfCFTA — cross-border demand for automation and energy engineering services

**Entry criteria:** proven industrial/agricultural electrical work from Phase 1
(control panels, pump systems), plus the data/monitoring competence from
Phase 2.

## Parallel Initiative — STEM Academy *(Status: Concept)*

A STEM education academy under the Giga Tech brand. Tracked separately from

## Guiding Principles

1. **Each phase pays for the next.** No phase launches on hope; it launches on the previous phase's cash flow and client base.
2. **List only what we can certify.** Reputation compounds faster than marketing in this market — one botched job undoes a year of flyers.
3. **Engineering is the moat.** Competitors sell panels; Giga Tech sells engineered systems. Every offering should make that visible.
4. **Installations are relationships.** The customer who calls for a DB board fault today is the 5kVA solar client next year and the SCADA client in five.
