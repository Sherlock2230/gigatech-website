Who we build for

Big operations. Bigger consequences when power fails.

When an outage stops irrigation, closes classrooms or halts production, the losses run into thousands per day. These systems are engineered to carry real industrial and institutional loads.

Farms & agro-processing

Boreholes, irrigation pumps, cold chain, milking parlours, incubators and grading sheds. Solar matches farming perfectly — your heaviest pumping loads run exactly when the sun is strongest.

Schools & clinics

Boarding schools, colleges and health facilities can't negotiate with load shedding. We keep classrooms, labs, kitchens, hostels, theatres and vaccine fridges powered — day and night.

Mines & factories

Processing plants, workshops, compressors, conveyors and site facilities. We design three-phase systems around motor starting currents and real duty cycles — sized for how industry actually draws power.

How a project runs

A process built for institutions, not impulse buys.

Large systems are decided in boardrooms and bursar's offices. Our process gives decision-makers the numbers and documents they need at every step.

1
Feasibility

Site audit & energy analysis

We audit your site, analyse your ZESA bills and metering data, and map your real load profile — including seasonal peaks and critical equipment.

2
Proposal

Engineered design & investment case

You receive a full technical proposal: system design, simulated performance, single-line diagrams, costing and payback projection — a document your board or financiers can act on.

3
Delivery

Phased installation & commissioning

We procure proven equipment, build in phases that fit your operations and budget, then test, commission and hand over with full documentation and training.

4
Support

Maintenance & monitoring

Scheduled maintenance and performance monitoring keep the system delivering what the design promised — for decades, not months.
